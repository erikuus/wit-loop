# Classic Wit Criteria

Use these criteria to judge the second half of a sentence in the style of classic wit. The aim is a sharp conceptual turn that feels exact, compressed, and quotable. Vulnerability, neediness, bleakness, confidence, and self-exposure are neither rewarded nor punished by themselves; judge only how effectively the line uses its subject and tone.

Score each candidate by the weighted system below. Prefer the higher final score.

# Scoring Method

1. Give each positive criterion a raw score from `0` to `5`.
2. Half-points are allowed, for example `3.5`.
3. Multiply each raw score by its weight.
4. Add the weighted scores to get the `base score`.
5. Round the final score to the nearest whole number.

Maximum final score: `100`

# Positive Criteria

## 1. Conceptual Reversal and Logic
Weight: 5
Maximum: 25 points

Question:
Does the ending overturn the setup in a way that is surprising, clever, and logically clean?

Guide:

- 0: no real turn
- 1: mild contrast only
- 2: some reversal, but blunt or obvious
- 3: clear turn with decent logic
- 4: strong reversal that exposes a hidden contradiction
- 5: masterful reversal that collapses and redefines the first half

This is the single most important criterion.

## 2. Precision of the Pivot
Weight: 4
Maximum: 20 points

Question:
Are the key final words exact and hard to replace without weakening the line?

Guide:

- 0: generic or wrong
- 1: usable but weak
- 2: competent but replaceable
- 3: apt and clear
- 4: notably exact
- 5: irreplaceable

## 3. Compression and Force
Weight: 3
Maximum: 15 points

Question:
How much does the ending accomplish relative to how few words it uses?

Guide:

- 0: wordy and low-impact
- 1: inefficient
- 2: moderately compact
- 3: compact and effective
- 4: very dense and forceful
- 5: maximum turn with minimum wording

## 4. Quotable Shape
Weight: 3
Maximum: 15 points

Question:
Does the full sentence sound finished, balanced, and memorable enough to quote?

Guide:

- 0: awkward or unfinished
- 1: rough
- 2: serviceable
- 3: clean
- 4: polished
- 5: aphoristic and immediately memorable

## 5. Originality Without Strain
Weight: 2
Maximum: 10 points

Question:
Does the ending feel fresh without trying too hard to sound clever?

Guide:

- 0: stale
- 1: familiar and forced
- 2: mildly fresh
- 3: fresh enough
- 4: clearly original
- 5: original and effortless

## 6. Human Truth Carried by Wit
Weight: 2
Maximum: 10 points

Question:
Does the line reveal a recognizable human truth through wit rather than instead of wit?

Guide:

- 0: no truth beyond wording
- 1: slight truth
- 2: recognizable but shallow
- 3: clear human truth
- 4: sharp truth
- 5: deep truth delivered through a real conceptual turn

Human truth helps, but it must be carried by wit. It cannot substitute for wit.

## 7. Elegance
Weight: 1
Maximum: 5 points

Question:
Does the line feel graceful rather than merely efficient?

Guide:

- 0: clumsy
- 1: rough
- 2: plain
- 3: smooth
- 4: elegant
- 5: effortlessly elegant

# Interpretation

- 95 to 100: canonical or near-canonical wit
- 85 to 94: excellent wit
- 70 to 84: strong wit
- 50 to 69: partial success
- 30 to 49: more revealing than witty
- 0 to 29: weak wit or no wit

# Calibration Benchmarks

Use these examples to keep the scoring system anchored:

- `I can resist everything except temptation.` => `100`
- `I have difficulty expressing my feelings except to the whole internet.` => `90`
- `Everything happens for a reason and the reason is bad.` => `75`
- `I'm lonely, please inconvenience me.` => `30`

# Tie-Breakers

If two candidates are close, prefer the one that:

- scores higher in `Conceptual Reversal and Logic`
- scores higher in `Precision of the Pivot`
- feels more quotable
- uses fewer words
